About me

I'm a postdoc in neuroscience living in Paris and London. My background is in psychology, neuroscience, software development and philosophy. My research aims at understanding the relationship between consciousness, social cognition and health at different behavioral and electrophysiological scales.

Interdisciplinary efforts are most fruitful when methods and applied researchers closely colaborate. With my partners from applied mathematics, computer science and medicine I therefore actively develop computational tools and methods.

I hope that this will help promote an active role for psychology in an increasingly technology-driven interdisciplinary future.

MNE-Python

MNE-Python is the Python open source toolbox for processing and visualizing MEG and EEG data. It facilitates a wide range of data processing task, including artifact rejection, diagnositc visualizations, decoding, source localization, time-frequency analysis and statistics.

I have been core developer since 2012.

  • http://martinos.org/mne/dev/index.html
  • https://github.com/mne-tools/mne-python
